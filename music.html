Gloria Liou (violin, 1996) is currently studying with Professor Sarah Thornblade of the Los Angeles Chamber Orchestra and previously studied with Professor Li Lin of the Juilliard School. Gloria is the concertmaster of the Pomona College Orchestra, and served as concertmaster of the California All-State Orchestra and the Saratoga High Orchestra.

Gloria has participated in numerous music competitions and festivals, and has received various awards. She placed 1st at the Claremont Colleges Concerto Competition (2015), 1st at the Nova Vista Symphony Young Artist Competition (2013), 1st at the CMTANC International Youth Music Competition (2013), 1st at the MTAC State Finals (2012), and 1st at the AFAF International Competition (2011), where she was invited to perform at Carnegie Hall. She soloed with the Nova Vista Symphony in 2014 and performed at the Junior Bach Festival in 2013 and 2011. Gloria also attended the Tanglewood Music Festival in 2012 and California Summer Music in 2011.

Gloria was the director of Music Education with Saratoga High (MESH), a mentoring community service program. With MESH, Gloria taught music students at local middle and elementary schools and coached young musicians for ensemble performances. Each year, the MESH Benefit Concert raises funds for Music Team SF to provide music education for students from low-income families and communities.
